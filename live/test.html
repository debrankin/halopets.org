Featured Grant Recipients

The Halo Pet Foundation™ announces nine non-profit organizations are awarded $55,000 in grants to continue their missions that focus on animal and human welfare as well as sustainability and the environment. Each of the grant recipients embody the mission of Halo Pet Foundation… to enhance the well being of pets and the people who love them by striving to help them live longer, happier, healthier lives together.

Global Animal Partnership (grant amount: $5,000)

A non-profit organization that brings together a diverse group (farmers, scientists, retailers, manufacturers and animal advocates) with the common goal of improving farm animal welfare standards around the world.

The Halo Pet Foundation's focus on responsible pet ownership, eliminating animal abuse and reducing overpopulation of uncared for pets is in keeping with GAP's goal of improving the lives of animals.

- Anne Malleau, Executive Director, GAP

Pet Sustainability Coalition (grant amount: $2,500)

Accelerate environmental and social sustainability in the pet industry through education, implementation tools and collaboration.

Through innovative industry collaboration like this we can accelerate the entire industry toward positive stewardship of our natural resources and global communities.

- Caitlyn Dudas, Executive Director, Pet Sustainability Coalition



Wings of Rescue (grant amount: $12,500)

Airlift large number of at-risk, healthy pets long distances from high-intake shelters to their network of shelters throughout the United States and Canada that have plenty of space and adopters waiting.

It is only natural that we partner with the Halo Foundation as we share the exact same moral code, trying to make the world a healthier and better place for pets – and their human families.

- Ric Browde, CEO, Wings of Rescue

Many and Friends Foundation (grant amount: $10,000)

From a child with a serious illness to a rescue organization helping save homeless pets, Manny harnesses the power of his passionate community to raise awareness and assist those who need the most help.

Lil BUB's Big Fund for the ASPCA (grant amount: $10,000)

Supports homeless pets with special needs nationwide.

National Bison Association (grant amount: $5,000)

The Bison 1 Million is a dedicated effort among ranchers, conservationists, tribal leaders and the public to restore one million bison to the rangelands of North America.

The Arizona Animal Welfare League (grant amount: $5,000)

Rescue, rehabilitate and rehome dogs, cats, puppies, kittens throughout the State of Arizona that have been surrendered, abused and neglected.

American Humane (grant amount: $2,500)

The Shelter to Service program provides highly trained, lifesaving service dogs to military veterans struggling with Post-Traumatic Stress and Traumatic Brain Injury.

The Sprouts Healthy Communities Foundation (grant amount: $2,500)

Supports health and wellness related causes that directly impact the neighborhoods where Sprouts customers and team members live, work and play.
